Impurity Limits — ICH Q3A(R2) / Q3B(R2)
Enter the maximum daily dose of the drug substance to calculate the reporting, identification ("unspecified impurity" limit), and qualification ("specified impurity" limit) thresholds — for the drug substance (Q3A) and the finished drug product (Q3B) in parallel.
Change molecule
MAXIMUM DAILY DOSE
How to read this: the identification threshold is the level above which an unspecified impurity/degradation product must be structurally identified — in practice, the working ceiling for any unspecified impurity in a specification. The qualification threshold is the level above which safety data are needed to justify (qualify) the impurity — the basis most commonly used to set the limit for a specified impurity. Where a guideline gives "X% or Y mg TDI, whichever is lower," the calculator applies both and reports the binding (lower) value. These are ICH thresholds, not a substitute for a full impurity risk assessment (including genotoxic/mutagenic impurities under ICH M7, which follow separate TTC-based limits).
Nitrosamine Risk — NDSRIs
Acceptable Intake (AI) limits for nitrosamine impurities, including the FDA's Carcinogenic Potency Categorization Approach (CPCA) for Nitrosamine Drug Substance-Related Impurities (NDSRIs) — plus a calculator to convert AI into a ppm control limit at your molecule's maximum daily dose.
Change molecule
What NDSRIs are: Nitrosamine Drug Substance-Related Impurities are nitrosamines that share structural similarity to the API itself (as opposed to small-molecule nitrosamines like NDMA/NDEA, which can arise generically from manufacturing). They typically form when a secondary or tertiary amine in the API structure reacts with a nitrosating agent during synthesis, storage, or formulation. If your API contains a secondary/tertiary amine — this is worth screening for regardless of therapeutic class.

Control limit (ppm)
Formula: ppm = AI (ng/day) ÷ MDD (mg/day) — this exact conversion, and the underlying units logic (ng/mg = ppm by mass), is stated directly in FDA's nitrosamine guidance. Analytical methods typically need low ppb-range limits of quantitation to reliably test against limits this tight.
Key dates worth tracking: FDA recommended NDSRI risk (re)assessments be completed by November 1, 2023, and confirmatory testing/application changes concluded by August 1, 2025 (per the Aug 2023 RAIL Guidance) — if your molecule wasn't screened against these deadlines, that's a gap worth closing regardless of when you're reading this.
